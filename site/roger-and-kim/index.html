Without Bullseye

Roger's day.
A flat list and a prayer.

8 AM
The Flat List Illusion
Every row looks identical.

Roger sits down with his coffee and opens a spreadsheet exported from Definitive Healthcare or IQVIA. He sees "Dr. Sarah M. (OBGYN)" and an NPI number. That's it. No intent signal. No practice context. No indicator of what she's actively marketing or which procedures she performs today.

The claims data driving that list is often 12 to 24 months old. Roger isn't working with intelligence. He's working with a census. And a census treats every name the same.

10 AM
The Targeting Tax
He's not selling.
He's researching.

Because the list is flat, Roger can't simply pick up the phone. He's forced to play part-time data analyst. He digs through practice websites, scans service pages, reads provider bios. He's trying to guess whether a clinic is actually a fit.

This manual verification consumes nearly 40% of his available selling time. Roughly 10 hours a week. Gone. Not to a competitor. To a spreadsheet he should never have been handed in the first place.

2 PM
The $300 Wasted Visit
He walks in.
And walks right back out.

Roger gets in his car and drives to a clinic that looked promising on paper. Time, mileage, and opportunity cost: approximately $300. He walks through the doors and finds a hard exclusion his list never flagged. An REI on staff protecting the IVF revenue stream. A telehealth-only operation with no procedure suite. A practice operating under a religious philosophy that rules out the device entirely.

And when he does find a viable prospect? He still walks in cold. No targeted clinical angle. Generic pitch language. A rep-shaped guess instead of a targeted consultation.

"I drove 45 minutes, paid for parking, and found out in the first 60 seconds that they don't perform any in-office procedures. It's not even in the database."

5 PM
The Inefficiency Tax
Exhausted.
Behind quota. Repeat.

Roger ends his day behind on the week. The list gave him 50 names. He burned 10 hours figuring out which ones were worth calling. He made 3 visits and got 1 real conversation. Multiply this across your entire field team, across 50 weeks. You're not looking at a performance problem. You're looking at a structural one.

Roger isn't failing because he can't sell. The targeting infrastructure is failing him. And it will keep failing him until someone changes the inputs.

Kim moves to her next target: score of 85, flagged as a strong fit. This is where she acts as the crucial check on the system. Because Bullseye is not a black box, every score comes with an Evidence Trace. Exact quoted text. Source URLs. The specific signals that drove the number.

She's a sales rep again. Not a researcher. Not a data clerk. A rep with a targeted brief, a clinical angle, and a pipeline she can actually defend.
