02 · The Targeting Tax

Name the hidden cost.
Win the conversation.

Opening Hook
Definition
Use this exact framing

"The targeting tax is the hidden cost of manual research. Your reps spend verifying practice fit, cross-referencing equipment signals, and guessing at business models instead of selling."

Where the Tax Shows Up
  • Invisible on the P&L. Nobody books rep research time as a line item. It shows up as quota miss and low pipeline velocity.
  • More data makes it worse. Every database license adds more records to sort manually. Without prioritization, it's just more noise.
  • Scales with headcount. At 4 reps losing 10 hrs/week that's 40 hrs of salary. At 12 reps, it's 120 hrs.
  • The defensibility gap. When pipeline misses, "we targeted the wrong accounts" is an uncomfortable answer. It is also an avoidable one.
The Numbers

The Fix
  • ✓Bullseye pre-scores practices before reps ever see the list
  • ✓Each target arrives with fit rationale, signal evidence, and a sales hook
  • ✓Research time drops to near zero. Selling time goes up.
  • ✓Reps inherit intelligence, not a list
03 · Objection Handling

The objections they'll raise.
The answers that land.

Field Ready
?"We already have ZoomInfo / Definitive Healthcare."
→Those tools give you coverage. They show who exists. Bullseye gives you prioritization. It tells you who is worth calling and gives your rep the reason why. They solve different problems. Most of our clients have both.
?"Is Bullseye just another AI dialer?"
→Not even close. Bullseye does not place calls, send emails, or touch your outreach workflow. We deliver research that is scored, sourced, and structured, so your reps know who to call before they pick up the phone.
?"What data do you use?"
→Only publicly observable signals from a physician or practice's online footprint: practice websites, directories, patient reviews, Google Business profiles. No claims data, no private records, no EMRs, no login-gated systems.
?"Does this replace our reps?"
→No. It makes them sharper. Bullseye tells reps where to focus and what to say. It eliminates the research drag so they spend more time on high-value conversations. Headcount stays the same; output goes up.
?"Is this compliant?"
→Yes. We analyze only publicly observable data: the public-facing footprint of a physician or practice. No PHI, no patient-level data, no claims files, no private databases. Every recommendation is evidence-backed and auditable.
?"Our reps already know how to research."
→They do. And that's exactly the problem. Every hour a rep spends researching is an hour not selling. We do that research before it reaches them so they can apply their judgment where it matters: on the call.

05 · Compliance & Data

What we use. What we don't.
How to say it.

Use Precisely
Exact sourcing statement. Use this verbatim.

"Bullseye analyzes publicly observable practice-level signals from a physician or practice's online footprint. We do not use patient-level data, claims files, medical records, appointment data, portals, EMRs, private databases, or login-gated systems."

What We Use
  • ✓Practice websites and public directories
  • ✓Google Business listings and patient reviews
  • ✓Public-facing physician/practice online footprint
  • ✓CMS utilization data where publicly available (Use sparingly. Default to "online footprint" language.)
  • ✓NPI registry: public and commercially permissible
What We Do NOT Use
  • ✕Patient records or protected health information (PHI)
  • ✕Claims files or medical records
  • ✕Appointment data or scheduling systems
  • ✕EMR or EHR systems
  • ✕Login-gated or paywalled sources
  • ✕Private databases of any kind
Language Rules: Say This, Not That
Cash-pay signal

✓ "Public cash-pay signal identified"
✕ "Cash-pay confirmed"

CRM delivery

✓ "CRM-import friendly, format confirmed per client"
✕ "Salesforce-ready"

Data accuracy

✓ "Observable signals, confidence rated"
✕ "Validated data" or "guaranteed accurate"

Cleanup promise

✓ "Designed to minimize cleanup"
✕ "Zero cleanup" or "no manual mapping"

Conversion claim

✓ "Stronger fit signal"
✕ "Guaranteed conversion"

Speed claim

✓ "Delivery in days, not weeks"
✕ "72-hour turnaround" (MVP constraint)
